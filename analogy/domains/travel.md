# travel — domain pack

> Travel runs in two registers. The **expedition / tour-operator / logistics-ops** register is built around permissions, sequencing, professional crews, and graceful failure under load — reach for it when the point is coordination, handoff, or a pipeline that has to hold under pressure. **Your own trip** is you packing a bag, winging the family holiday, or taking the same road you always take — reach for it when the point is one person's judgment, a habit you carry, or something that quietly accumulates. Both map onto how an AI coding session runs; offer both and let the concept pick.

---

## Register axis

This pack carries two registers. Offer both — the model picks the right one per concept *when both are on the table*.

| Register | Feel | Reach for it when the concept is about… |
|---|---|---|
| **Expedition / tour-operator / logistics-ops** (subsystem) | a professional operation under pressure, fleets, itineraries, specialist crew | permissions, sequencing, handoff, who-owns-which-checkpoint, pipeline resilience |
| **Your own trip** (everyday) | one person packing a bag, the family holiday, the road trip, the route you always take | a single judgment, a habit you carry on every trip, what you pack by default, what accumulates from experience |

**Selection cue:** *subsystem when the point is coordination across legs or stages; everyday when the point is one person's choice or something that builds up quietly.* Never mix the two in one breath — offer both in the pack, pick one in the sentence.

## Freeform mappings

**Expedition / logistics-ops** (coordination under load):

- **The gate** — where the next decision fires. Everything before it is preparation; everything after it is consequence.
- **The connection** — the most fragile bit. One delay cascades into a re-plan.
- **Standby** — held but not approved. The permission you might get if conditions clear.
- **TSA Pre-check / Global Entry** — stored permissions. The speed-pass past the recurring check, *because the check already happened once*.
- **The gate change** — late-breaking drift signal. Plan was good at booking, no longer holds at boarding.
- **The layover** — built-in slack for graceful failure. Tight connection = no slack = fragile.
- **Lost baggage** — silent failure, recovered after the fact. The pipeline kept going without it.
- **Re-routing** — rebuilding the plan mid-journey when the original breaks.
- **The boarding sequence** — strict ordering with consequences if you miss your slot.
- **The transit lounge** — held in-between state; not in the source country, not in the destination, governed by neither.
- **The visa stamp** — durable permission with an expiry. Different from the boarding pass (single-use).

**Your own trip** (one traveller, repetition, accumulation):

- **What's already in your bag** — the tools and context already loaded; work with what you brought, not the ideal kit list.
- **The route you always take** — a cached result you reach for by habit; not the optimal path, just the known one.
- **Winging it** — no itinerary, judgment call at each junction. Works fine for a short hop; fragile across multiple legs.
- **The thing that went wrong last time** — accumulated trip memory; the lesson carried forward into packing or planning.
- **Overhead bin space** — a limited shared resource, first-come first-served, with real consequences for anyone who boards late.
- **The family-holiday compromise** — the plan nobody fully wanted but everyone agreed on. The constraint-driven version of the ideal trip.
- **Packing light vs packing safe** — the evergreen tradeoff: travel lean and fast, or carry everything just in case. Maps onto minimal-context vs full-preload decisions.

## When the analogy doesn't fit

- **Long-term ownership / contractual problems.** Use construction. Travel is transactional.
- **Aesthetic / craft / brand work.** Use cooking. Travel is procedural.
- **Day-to-day household ops.** Use home. Travel is episodic; home is continuous.
- **Adversarial-only frames.** Travel imports authority-relations (gates, officers) that can over-formalise collaborative work.
- **Don't mix the two registers in one breath.** Expedition *or* your-own-trip per concept, never "the tour operator packed the wrong bag." Offer both in the pack, pick one in the sentence.
