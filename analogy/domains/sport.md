# sport — domain pack

> Sport runs in two registers. The **pro / elite squad** is structured, season-long, and unambiguous about who owns which position — reach for it when the point is coordination under live load, role specialisation, and what happens when one position breaks down. **Amateur / weekend sport** is one person or a loose group deciding for themselves: the kickabout in the park, the local league, keeping fit on your own — reach for it when the point is a single habit, a low-stakes attempt, or something that quietly accumulates through repetition. Both map onto how an AI coding session runs; offer both and let the concept pick.

> Value-alignment note (per §12): sport imports a win/lose frame. That's correct for some problems and quietly poisonous for others. Don't reach for sport when the work is collaborative, exploratory, or zero-sum thinking would distort the call.

---

## Register axis

This pack carries two registers. Offer both — the model picks the right one per concept *when both are on the table* (validated on the §10.5 "hooks-as-team-sport" case, where one response drew elite-squad for a handoff and weekend-amateur for a single training habit without the two bleeding together).

| Register | Feel | Reach for it when the concept is about… |
|---|---|---|
| **Pro / elite squad** (subsystem) | a professional outfit under match pressure, many players in parallel | hierarchy, handoff, role specialisation, who-covers-what, live coordination |
| **Amateur / weekend** (everyday) | one person or a loose group deciding for themselves, the same run on repeat | a single person's habit, low-stakes repetition, what accumulates through practice, the informal version |

**Selection cue:** *pro / elite squad when the point is coordination under live load; amateur / weekend when the point is one habit or something that builds through repetition.* When in doubt offer both terms and let the sentence pick — but never mix the two in one breath (no "the head coach squeezes in a quick jog before the team talk").

## Roles (who does what)

| Concept | Pro / elite squad | Amateur / weekend | Reach for… |
|---|---|---|---|
| User | Captain (on the pitch) / head coach (above it) — the decision-maker in or over the game | The person who organised the kickabout — has to actually play, and picks the teams | pro for sign-off + hierarchy; amateur for "lives with the result" |
| Expert (any tier) | Position player / specialist (striker, defender, keeper) — owns one position, doesn't drift across the pitch | The friend who's just better at one sport — called for that one thing | pro when positions matter; amateur for one-off borrowed know-how |
| Meta-expert | Head coach / manager — sets the system, doesn't take the kicks | Whoever got you into running — shaped your habits, isn't there on the day | pro for structure and system; amateur for inherited instinct |
| Adversarial reviewer | Match referee / opposition scout / video analyst — catches what the team normalised | The blunt friend on the touchline who says "you keep dropping your shoulder" | pro for formal review; amateur for an honest gut check |
| Subagent | Substitute / training-ground player — on the bench, ready, not in play | A mate who might join later — available, not committed yet | pro in live play; amateur for a loosely available helper |
| Domain expert | Specialist coach (set-piece, goalkeeping, fitness) — one narrow craft, deep practice | The local club coach who knows one drill really well | pro for a named specialism under pressure; amateur for borrowed technique |
| Sub-expert pair | Two players competing for the same shirt — deliberately differing on one axis | Two friends who always argue about the route or the pace | either — two voices on one thing, deliberately differing |

## Rhythms (how time and pace work)

| Concept | Pro / elite squad | Amateur / weekend | Reach for… |
|---|---|---|---|
| AWE (token effort) | Minutes on the pitch + energy spent — the cost extracted from the squad | The effort of showing up, doing the thing, getting home | either — the real cost of doing the thing |
| Session | A match — one bounded contest | One run, one game, one gym slot | pro for a bounded team contest; amateur for one personal outing |
| Session-open | Pre-match warm-up + team talk — last reads before kick-off | Lacing up and checking the weather before heading out | pro for the team brief; amateur for the solo check |
| Session-close | Final whistle + cool-down + debrief — the match ends, state persists into the next | Stretching, noting how it went, leaving the kit by the door | either — tidy, restock, note for next time |
| Calibration cycle | Mid-season review — step back, audit form across multiple matches | "I've not improved in months — time to change the routine" | pro for a squad-level rethink; amateur for a personal habit review |
| Coaching pass | One-on-one with the position coach — targeted feedback on one player | A friend watching your form and telling you what's off | pro for structured targeted feedback; amateur for borrowed insight |
| Drift | Tactical drift across matches — the system being played isn't the system on the whiteboard | The run that's quietly become a shuffle | pro for spec deviation; amateur for habit drift |
| Cross-triage | Pundit / opposition scout's view — outside eyes on the team's shape | Asking someone who doesn't play with you what they notice | pro for a formal outside read; amateur for a second pair of eyes |

## Verbs (what people do)

| Concept | Pro / elite squad | Amateur / weekend | Reach for… |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recommend | Make the pass / call the play — put the move on | "I'd try that route" | pro for sending it; amateur for suggesting it |
| Veto | "No, hold position" — don't make that move | "Not doing that again" | pro for a tactical block; amateur for a hard personal no |
| Defer | "Wait for the run" — hold, the right window hasn't opened | "Let's see how the legs feel first" | either — wait, don't fire yet |
| Approve | "Go!" — green-light the move | "Yeah, let's do it" | pro for the tactical green light; amateur for a casual yes |
| Calibrate | Tactical adjustment at halftime — adjust the shape based on what's happening | Slowing down because you went out too fast | either — adjust the plan to what's actually happening |
| Iterate | Try a different formation — same opponent, new system | Do the session again, fix what didn't work | either — same goal, different approach |
| Build (new expert) | Bring on a new player — sub in / sign in the transfer window | Invite someone new to the group who's good at one thing | pro for a new squad role; amateur for a new regular |
| Retire | Drop / transfer out — off the squad, that role passes to someone else | Stop running, take up cycling instead | pro for closing a position; amateur for dropping a habit |
| Adversarial pass | Tactical analysis session — bring in someone outside the dressing room | Ask the friend who never praises you to watch you play | pro for a formal outside review; amateur for a blunt taster |
| Stance protocol | Attack / defend / control — three modes every passage of play falls into | Push it / pace yourself / just finish | either — three surfaces every session touches |

## Freeform mappings

**Pro / elite squad** (coordination under live load):

- **The pitch** — the live arena. Everything in the analysis room is dress rehearsal until play starts here. Maps onto the open session.
- **The bench** — your reserves; not in play but available. Maps onto sub-agents kept warm.
- **The dressing room** — internal space, not for spectators. The pre-match team-talk and the post-match honesty both live here.
- **Set pieces** — pre-built routines that fire on a known trigger (corners, free-kicks). Maps onto skills and hooks; the team rehearsed them precisely so they don't have to think when the trigger comes.
- **The transfer window** — limited time to reshape the squad. Outside the window, you play with what you've got.
- **The captain's armband** — pinned authority on the pitch. The manager still owns tactics; the captain owns moments.
- **The video room** — post-match adversarial review. Where the team gets honest about what actually happened.
- **The training ground** — where new patterns get rehearsed before they reach the pitch. Stuff that fails here is cheap; stuff that fails on the pitch is not.
- **Marking** — assigned responsibility for a specific risk (one player, one zone, one threat). Maps onto trigger-guards and ownership.
- **Off the ball** — what's happening when the action's elsewhere. Often where the real work is — the runs that create space, the cover that doesn't need to be used.
- **The bench-clear moment** — the formation collapses, everyone reacts; rare, costly, mostly a sign of upstream failure.

**Amateur / weekend** (one person, repetition, accumulation):

- **The kickabout** — unstructured, low-stakes, self-organising. The throwaway-POC path: no match plan, just see what happens.
- **Your usual route** — the run you do without thinking. The kept habit, the same path taken enough times that the decision is automatic.
- **Keeping a training log** — writing down what you did, how it felt. Maps onto working memory and the post-session close: small notes that compound.
- **A personal best** — not beating anyone else, just doing better than last time. Calibration without competition.
- **Showing up anyway** — when the conditions aren't ideal but you do the session regardless. The discipline that accumulates silently.
- **Turning back early** — knowing your limit mid-session and cutting it short. The honest stop before drift turns into injury. Maps onto the session-lifespan cap.

## When this analogy doesn't fit

- **Long-cycle structural decisions.** Use construction. Sport is sessional, not durable.
- **Aesthetic / craft work.** Use cooking. Sport is procedural and adversarial.
- **Solo / sequential tasks.** The pro / elite squad register assumes a team and concurrent play — switch to amateur / weekend if it's one person working alone.
- **Collaborative, exploratory, non-zero-sum work.** Sport imports win/lose framing that will distort the call. This is a §12 value-alignment failure, not a structural one.
- **Reflective / slow work.** The pro register runs at match-pace. Reaching for it can over-accelerate decisions that warrant pause. The amateur register is slower — prefer it for gradual, accumulating work.
- **Don't mix the two registers in one breath.** Pro *or* amateur per concept, never "the head coach squeezes in a quick jog before the team talk." Offer both in the pack; pick one in the sentence.
