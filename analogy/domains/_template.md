Name both, not just the impressive one.)

## Contract

A domain pack must cover four sections at minimum (Roles / Gear / Rhythms / Verbs), each in the dual-register shape below, plus a `## Register axis` block. Below them, freeform.

## Register axis

State the two registers this domain carries and a one-line cue for picking between them. The default failure mode is **subsystem-monoculture** — writing the whole pack in the impressive professional register (brigade, big-site contractor, elite team) and leaving the everyday register (home cook, DIY-er, weekend amateur) empty. Offer both; the model picks the right one per concept *when both are on the table*.

| Register | Feel | Reach for it when the concept is about… |
|---|---|---|
| **<subsystem>** (subsystem) | a professional operation under pressure, many hands in parallel | hierarchy, handoff, time-pressure, who-owns-what, parallel work |
| **<everyday>** (universe) | one person deciding for themselves, the same thing on repeat | a single judgment, everyday repetition, what accumulates, the artefact you keep |

**Selection cue:** *subsystem when the point is coordination; everyday when the point is one judgment or something that accumulates.* Never mix the two in one breath — offer both in the pack, pick one in the sentence.
